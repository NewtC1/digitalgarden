#tainted_grail 
The first Tainted Grail game released on Steam. This game is a roguelike with similar gameplay to [[Slay the Spire\|Slay the Spire]]. It uses the repeated runs to build upon the lore of the Tainted Grail: Fall of Avalon board game. The story takes place on a liminal space trapped in the [[Wyrdness\|Wyrdness]]. Players are guided by a strange goatman who appears at key story moments, as well as appearances from Death.

Characters in the game are NPCs that the player can find and recruit from their journeys into the [[Wyrdness\|Wyrdness]]. These NPCs have various upgrades to make subsequent runs easier, as well as quests that unlock further permanent upgrades.

Additionally, runs are improved by adding more cards to the drop lists and unlocking other classes, which play differently. The goal of each run is to defeat all four bosses and complete various side quests to unlock the true ending.

# Story
The game opens with the goatman running the player character through the process of fighting, and pitting them against the Fore-dweller that later becomes the final boss for a normal run. After dying to the Fore-dweller, they introduce the player to the town they start in, where NPCs are located and tells them to go kill the guardians to resolve all the [[Wyrdness\|Wyrdness]] business.

Killing the first guardian has him reappearing and commenting that this was all started when the humans invaded Avalon, starting a war and corrupting the world when they attempted to use the [[Wyrdness\|Wyrdness]] themselves. Human emotions unfortunately seeped into the mists and gave it a corruptive effect, causing the eventual loss of the developed lands.

Killing the second guardian causes the goatman to comment that this one had promise, and he's surprised we got this far. There's some hint that the guardian was another player character that we just slew.

Killing the third guardian brings little more insight, with him simply treating it as an inevitability at this point.

The fourth guardian is the same [[Fore-dwellers\|Fore-dweller]] we fought during the tutorial. After killing him, the Goatman reveals that there is an ongoing story that both the player and the Fore-dweller are locked in. The two sides are running through this story over and over, always battling each other at the end. They are good and evil, and from both perspectives they are the hero of the story. He unlocks the ability to fight the final boss by collecting the three Guardian hearts and sacrificing them at the Menhir in the center of the town. Doing so allows the player to fight the Fore-dweller Overlord, who can't actually be defeated but tracks the amount of damage done to it.

Surviving the 15 turns mandated by the fight prompts Death to step in. Death, furious with the Goat for letting you get this far, reveals that the story the player and Fore-dweller are locked in has a purpose. Trying to circumvent that purpose would damage the world, something the Goat hinted might happen before the player fights the Overlord. Death however gives us a new task: we are to save all of the NPCs from corruption. When that is done, we can make more progress.
# NPCs
- Town
	- Man-at-arms
		- Teaches the character new skills, allows them to manage their deck.
		- Refused to train a youth, and was imprisoned instead.
			- This youth was later revealed to be The Squire, who is trying to gather rocks to build his kingdom in the [[Wyrdness\|Wyrdness]]. Man-at-arms mentions that he refused to train him because the Squire grew crueler over time, and he didn't want to give him more tools to hurt others. He didn't take action though because he still respected the boy's father, who was the lord of the lands.
		- Occasionally we can find another man-at-arms in the [[Wyrdness\|Wyrdness]] who was his friend.
	- Blacksmith
		- First NPC you find.
		- Name is Wayland.
		- Works on runestones
		- Doesn't make weapons
		- Obsessed with a girl called [[Silence\|Silence]] who turns into a swan when encountered in the [[Wyrdness\|Wyrdness]]
			- Finding [[Silence\|Silence]] confirms that the Blacksmith wasn't the cause of the failing weapons in the war. He was instead sabotaged by a rival, which drew Silence to him as she feasted on his emotions.
		- Accused of treason
	- Scientist
		- Thaumaturge who works on potions
		- Name is Hilde
		- Cast out from her village for her experiments
		- Taken in by a man who encouraged her experimes.
			- We can find the man's house, destroyed in the [[Wyrdness\|Wyrdness]]. It turns out that one of Hilde's experiments started a fire which led to the entire house burning and killing him. The Seamstress will come to comfort Hilde at this point. She reveals how the man used Hilde to make money, recognizing her talent. This allows her to move on from his death.
		- Her tribe did a LOT of raiding of other tribes
		- Allows the player to both find and buy consumable items.
	- Candlemaker
		- Creates [[Wyrdcandles\|Wyrdcandles]]
			- Notably does NOT use humans for his tallow, but acknowledges the candles are made from wyrdspawn, which might have once been humans.
		- Name is Lugh
		- Repeatedly slandered because people saw his craft as a threat to their own rule.
			- One of the people who slandered him can be found in the [[Wyrdness\|Wyrdness]] as a massive monster. He ate the other members of the traveling troupe, turning him into the grotesque form we encounter. Lugh eventually reveals that he was a manipulative monster even before being claimed by the Wyrdness. In saying, he understands that the slander against him wasn't his fault.
		- Involved in freeing an elderly archer in the Wyrdness.
	- Ghost
		- Gambler, found trapped in an inn that repeats events based on his cards. Convincing him to allow the events to play out results in him accepting death, a fate he becomes quite happy with.
		- Allows player to gamble with gold, possibly allowing them to gain quite a lot at the start of a run.
		- Also allows player to convert experiences encountered in the [[Wyrdness\|Wyrdness]] to other currencies for other NPCs.
	- Lost Soul
		- First NPC encountered.
		- Gives the player the major quests.
		- Allows player to upgrade their healing flasks, a limited item given at the start of each run.
		- Last NPC to be saved, as all other NPCs need to be saved before he starts his quest.
			- This NPC is revealed to be Merlin himself.
	- Seamstress
		- Name is Neith.
		- Wyrdtouched, has needles for fingers
		- Protected by a gigantic spidery "Lady" who she helps to give birth.
			- Her questline has us providing the Lady with offerings of pure blood at various shrines. By doing this, we ensure the Lady's offspring won't grow up wyrdtainted, leading to her at least accepting us as an alternative protector for Neith. After that, Neith has us find the Countess and ask her for a piece of her skin, Neith then turns into a dress for the Protector. This done, the Protector officially starts guarding the town.
- Story
	- Goatman
		- Guides the player through the story.
		- A servant of Death?
		- Doesn't seem to want to follow Death's orders perfectly.
	- Death
		- Yes, the real Death.
		- This guy has one very funny quest where he gets summoned by a bunch of cultists. If the player manages to get into the ritual, he notices us and, given our inability to die, finds it extremely funny. He gives us the option to decide the fate of the cultists. Does he kill them for the audacity of summoning literally Death, or does he spare them?
	- Bard
		- Man who's looking for his brother. He can teach many songs. If we find his spear, we can give it to him which causes the Ghost to get pissed off. Turns out the brother's soul was in the spear, and can't be heard by the Bard -- but can be heard by the Ghost. We tell the Bard to make the spear into an instrument, allowing him to be heard again.
	- Witch
		- Trapped in the [[Wyrdness\|Wyrdness]], but displaced from space. If we help her we can visit her village and are given the option to destroy it. Doing so harms us, as it reveals that while we can see and interact with the village, it's location, much like the Witch's, is displaced. We only see an image. Kinda reminds me of [[The Aleph\|The Aleph]].
	- Florist
		- Man trapped in the [[Wyrdness\|Wyrdness]] who is selling fleshy flower plants. He gives the player a bouquet to give to "a lady". We can offer it to numerous people, but almost all of them turn it down.
	- Provider
		- An old woman followed by a caravan of (mostly) undead. Don't know much about her yet.
